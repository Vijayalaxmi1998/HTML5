In a sense,   India did create history via Chandrayaan 3 in that it was the first country to land on the South Pole of the lunar surface. That is something no one has attempted till date. Exactly 20 minutes prior to the landing time, ISRO initiated its Automatic Landing Sequence (ALS) . This well-tested and iterated process enabled Vikram LM to take charge and use its on-board computers and logic to identify a favourable spot. Not surprisingly, Chandrayaan 3 had a soft and decisive landing on the moon. In a sense, it was a redemption for Dr Sivan, who had seen the Chandrayaan 2 mission fail by a whisker in last few minutes of landing.
